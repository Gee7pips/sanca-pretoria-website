## 4. HISTORY & HERITAGE

### SANCA National Timeline

| Year | Event |
|---|---|
| **1956** | SANCA National established. First conference opened on 3 February 1956 by the Mayor of Johannesburg. First Executive Committee formed; first President: Mr R.P. Anderson; Vice-President: Mr G.A.C. Kuschke. Registered as a Welfare Organisation in 1956. |
| **1957** | First affiliates approved at the second conference, including the **Pretoria Society** — one of eight founding affiliated societies. Others: Brakpan, Durban, Cape Peninsula, Johannesburg, Springs, Vereeniging, Welkom. |
| **1970** | SANCA expanded its mandate from Alcoholism only to include all drugs and substances. |
| **Present** | 32 SANCA Societies operational across all 9 provinces. SANCA Pretoria is one of the largest and most established local societies. |

### SANCA Pretoria Specific
- Pretoria Society was one of the **original 8 founding affiliates** in 1957.
- Has operated in Pretoria for approximately **68+ years.**
- Operates **3 distinct clinics** covering a geographic spread from Pretoria North to Soshanguve and Hammanskraal.
- Castle Carey Clinic is situated in a **"peaceful Pretoria suburb"** and welcomes family members as part of the recovery process.

## 12. DRUG INFORMATION CONTENT

*Source: sancapta.co.za/drug-info/ (captured via Google index) — the Drug Info page categorises substances as: Opioids, Hallucinogens, Stimulants & Depressants*

---

### Dagga (Cannabis / Marijuana)

**Classification:** Depressant / Hallucinogen
**Source:** Derived from cannabinoid plant *Cannabis Sativa* — contains chemicals that slow down the Central Nervous System, affecting the brain, heart, and lungs.
**Active chemical:** THC — stays in the blood for weeks as it is stored in fat.

**How it's used:**
- Smoked in hand-rolled joints or 'slowboat'
- Smoked in a pipe
- Mixed with Mandrax/Heroin and smoked in a bottleneck ("White pipe")
- Brewed as tea or mixed into food
- Sold in plastic bank bags called 'bankies' or compressed into dagga parcels and bricks

**Short-term effects:**
Bloodshot red eyes, increased sleep, mood changes, coughing, dry mouth, increased appetite, memory loss, impaired judgement and false reality.

**Withdrawal symptoms:**
Cravings, thirst, dry mouth or throat, increased appetite, strange or vivid dreams, irritability and anger, restlessness, nervousness, depression and anxiety.

---

### Heroin

**Classification:** Opioid
**Source:** Made from Morphine — a natural substance taken from the seed pod of opium poppy plants.

**How it's used:**
- Injected
- Sniffed / snorted
- Smoked
- Mixed with Crack Cocaine (called "speedballing")

**Short-term effects:**
Dry mouth, warm flushing of skin, heavy feeling in arms and legs, nausea, vomiting, severe itching, clouded mental functioning, back-and-forth state of consciousness and semi-consciousness, slowed or slurred speech.

**Withdrawal symptoms:**
Restlessness, severe muscle and joint pain, sleep problems, diarrhoea and vomiting, cold flashes with goose bumps, uncontrollable leg movements.

**Long-term effects:**
Insomnia, collapsed veins (for injectors), damaged tissue inside nose (for snorters), constipation and stomach cramping, infection of the heart lining and valves, abscesses, liver and kidney disease, lung complications (including pneumonia), mental disorders, sexual dysfunction for men, coma, death.

---

### Methamphetamine (Tik / Crystal Meth)

**Classification:** Stimulant
**Street names:** Tik, Crystal Meth, Ice, Speed

**How it's used:**
- Snorting (inhaling through nose)
- Smoking
- Injecting

**Short-term effects:**
High energy levels, over-alertness, increased physical activity, uncontrollable jerking body movements, euphoria.

**Withdrawal / dependency risks:**
Insomnia, hallucinations, anxiety, paranoia. In some cases, convulsions that can lead to death.

**Long-term effects:**
Increased heart rate and blood pressure, brain damage, liver, kidney and lung damage, severe tooth decay (meth mouth), depression.

> **Note:** Current website spells this as "methemphatemines" — **must be corrected to "methamphetamines"** on new site.

---

### Mandrax (Methaqualone)

**Classification:** Depressant / Sedative
**Form:** Mostly tablet form
**Common use:** Tablet crushed and mixed with Dagga, smoked in a Dagga pipe or "Bottleneck" — known as the "White pipe"

**Signs of use:**
Appears tired and sleepy or restless; poor co-ordination; slurring of words.

**Dependency risks:**
Rapid weight loss, headaches, serious emotional problems, depression, insomnia, epilepsy, impairment of liver function, emphysema and chronic lung infection, fatal thrombosis, convulsions.

---

### Cocaine / Crack Cocaine

**Classification:** Stimulant
**Forms:**
- Cocaine: fine white, odourless, bitter-tasting crystalline powder — commonly sniffed
- Crack: crystalised rock form — often smoked

**Short-term effects:**
Feelings of happiness and excitement, self-confidence, alertness, hallucination (at high dose).

---

### Alcohol

**Classification:** Depressant
**Admission type:** Special admission hours at Castle Carey Clinic (06:00–16:00 and 19:00–22:00 daily, 7 days a week)
**Note:** Alcohol is specifically addressed with dedicated admission slots, indicating it is a primary presenting substance.

## APPENDIX B — STATISTICS & CONTEXT (South Africa)

*For use in website content, blog, and drug information pages:*

- **Drug addiction poses significant challenges in South Africa**, impacting individuals, families, and communities nationwide.
- The increasing use of substances like marijuana, methamphetamines (Tik), and opioids has led to a rise in crime, poverty rates and health complications.
- Substance abuse **worsens chronic illnesses** like AIDS and tuberculosis (TB), as well as increasing rates of injury and premature death.
- Substance abuse has significant **economic effects**, including reduced productivity, increased absenteeism, financial difficulties, potential job loss, increased crime, and unexpected health expenses.
- Primary illicit substances at admission to South African drug treatment centres (SACENDU data): Cannabis 16.9%, Tik/Methamphetamine 12.8%, Crack/Cocaine 9.6%, Heroin/Opiates 9.2%, Cannabis + Mandrax 3.4%, Prescription/OTC 2.6%.
- **32 SANCA Societies** operate in most larger centres across South Africa — at least one in all 9 provinces.
